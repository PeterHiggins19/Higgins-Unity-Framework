# CoDaWork 2026 — Who's Who

## Know the room before you walk in

---

### Vera Pawlowsky-Glahn
**University of Girona, Spain**
- Co-architect of modern Aitchison geometry with Egozcue
- Co-author of the standard textbook (Wiley, 2015)
- Senior figure in CoDa — if she speaks, the room listens
- **Her work that matters to you:** Algebraic-geometric structure of the simplex
- **If she asks you something:** Answer carefully and precisely. She is exact.
- **Good opening:** "Your textbook was essential to my preparation."

### Juan José Egozcue
**Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC), Barcelona**
- The other half of the Pawlowsky-Egozcue partnership
- Developed the ILR transformation
- Thinks in Hilbert spaces
- **Already engaged with you.** He read your packet. He accepted your abstract. He's presenting a poster on Aitchison norm / concentration — the K_eff bridge.
- **Your relationship:** He is the door that opened. Treat with highest respect.
- **He already told you:** Use perturbation not subtraction. Don't emphasize constant sum. Drop HUF acronyms. Link to Aitchison norm.

### Javier Palarea-Albaladejo
**BioSS (Biomathematics & Statistics Scotland)**
- The zero problem expert
- Author of `zCompositions` R package
- **He WILL ask:** "How do you handle zeros in your EMBER data?"
- **Your answer:** "Structural zeros flagged as genuine absence. I haven't applied zCompositions methods yet. That's a gap I'd value your advice on."
- **Bonus move:** If you've downloaded and looked at zCompositions before the conference, mention it. He'll appreciate that you read his work.

### Jan Graffelman
**UPC Barcelona**
- Compositional biplots, genetics, Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium
- Strong on visualization
- **Connection to your work:** Your CoDa Explorer panels are visualization tools. He may have ideas on better compositional visualization.
- **Good question to ask him:** "What would a compositional biplot of my EMBER data show that my CLR time series doesn't?"

### Josep Antoni Martín-Fernández
**University of Girona**
- Zero replacement methods, Bayesian approaches
- Collaborates with Palarea-Albaladejo
- **He cares about:** Count zeros, imputation, the boundary of the simplex
- **Your connection:** The structural zeros in EMBER data are his territory too

### Karel Hron
**Palacký University, Olomouc, Czech Republic**
- ILR coordinates, functional compositional data
- Bridging CoDa with functional data analysis
- **Why he matters:** If your compositional time series is viewed as functional data on the simplex, his methods apply directly
- **Good question:** "Would functional CoDa methods capture the smooth drift my protocol detects as perturbation velocity?"

### Peter Filzmoser
**TU Wien, Vienna**
- Robust statistics for compositional data
- Outlier detection on the simplex
- **Direct connection:** Your regime boundaries (Fukushima spike, German nuclear exit) are compositional outliers. His methods could formalize what your protocol detects informally.
- **Good question:** "Would your robust outlier detection flag the same regime boundaries my perturbation velocity identifies?"

### Raimon Tolosana-Delgado
**Helmholtz-Zentrum, Freiberg, Germany**
- Geosciences applications
- Co-author of the Wiley textbook
- **Connection:** He applies CoDa to real-world geoscience data. He understands the gap between theory and application. Likely sympathetic to your applied approach.

## THE GOLDEN RULE

Every person in this room has published more papers than you. Every person knows more CoDa mathematics than you. None of them have operated a physical compositional system for 40 years. Lead with what they know. Offer what they don't have. Learn everything you can.
