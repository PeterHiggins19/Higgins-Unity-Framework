KEY FINDINGS
============

1. TEMPORAL EITT HOLDS (Even/Odd Half-Mission Split)
   Full map mean H:       0.442011
   Even-ring mean H:      0.441911  (Δ = -0.023%)
   Odd-ring mean H:       0.440575  (Δ = -0.325%)

   The even-ring and odd-ring half-mission maps — which represent
   interleaved temporal subsets of Planck's scanning — preserve
   compositional entropy to within 0.3%. This is temporal EITT applied
   to cosmological observation data.

   VERDICT: TEMPORAL EITT HOLDS at 0.3% (2:1 decimation)

2. SPATIAL EITT FAILS (Pixel Grouping Decimation)
   4:1 spatial decimation:    +5.2%
   16:1 spatial decimation:   +9.6%
   4096:1 spatial decimation: +18.1%

   Entropy INCREASES monotonically with spatial coarse-graining.

   THE FAILURE IS DIAGNOSTIC: It quantifies spatial heterogeneity.
   The sky is NOT compositionally homogeneous — the galactic plane
   (10-14% polarization) is structurally different from the polar
   caps (32-38% polarization). Spatial decimation mixes structurally
   different sky regions, increasing entropy.

   This is the SPATIAL analogue of the gold/silver temporal EITT failure:
   spatial heterogeneity plays the role of temporal non-stationarity.

3. NOISE BIAS vs ENTROPY INVARIANCE
   Global mean polarization: Full=26.8%, Even=36.1%, Odd=36.4%

   The half-maps show ~10% higher polarization fraction than the full map.
   This is the known noise bias in CMB polarimetry: P=sqrt(Q²+U²) is
   positively biased, and half-maps have √2 more noise per pixel.

   Despite this noise bias changing the polarization fraction by ~35%,
   the ENTROPY changes by only 0.3%. The compositional structure is
   preserved even when noise levels change substantially.

   This separation of signal (entropy-preserving structure) from noise
   (amplitude-changing bias) is precisely what EITT was designed to detect.

4. GALACTIC LATITUDE STRUCTURE
   North polar cap:  32-39% polarization, H ~ 0.63-0.67
   Equatorial (plane): 11-14% polarization, H ~ 0.34-0.41
   South polar cap:  32-34% polarization, H ~ 0.63-0.64

   The galactic plane has 3x more total intensity but 3x less polarization
   fraction than the polar caps. This is physically expected: the plane
   contains more dust but with more tangled magnetic field geometry,
   reducing net polarization.

   The entropy gradient from equator to poles (0.34 → 0.67) traces the
   magnetic field coherence structure of the Milky Way.

SIGNIFICANCE
============

This is the first application of CoDa compositional methods to CMB data.

For HUF/CoDa:
- Temporal EITT confirmed in a 5th distinct domain (cosmological)
- Spatial EITT failure provides a diagnostic tool for spatial structure
- The separation of noise bias from compositional structure validates
  EITT as a signal/noise discriminator

For Cosmology:
- CoDa provides a rigorous framework for analyzing polarization as
  a composition (the fraction of emission that is polarized)
- The entropy gradient across the sky is a new way to map magnetic
  field coherence
- Scale-invariance of temporal observations (even/odd split) connects
  to the standard assumption of instrument stability
