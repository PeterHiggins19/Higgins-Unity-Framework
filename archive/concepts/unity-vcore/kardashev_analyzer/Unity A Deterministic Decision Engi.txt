Abstract
Complex decision systems—from municipal net-zero pathways to planetary resource allocation—require frameworks balancing infinite possibilities with practical, traceable outcomes. Traditional optimization suffers stochastic noise or unbounded drift; performance hierarchies (e.g., Bhutan GPMS) excel alignment but lack dynamic trade-offs.

We introduce Unity, a novel primitive operating as a deterministic projector in infinite-dimensional Hilbert space. The full possibility space comprises all conceivable regimes (energy, mobility, economic, social, environmental, planetary) as basis vectors. Unity enforces exact normalization (unity-sum invariant) and determinism via forced truncation to significance: Low-gauge (uncertain) dimensions discarded, projecting to finite significant subspace. Evolution is bounded hill-climb or Pareto search (NSGA-II/SPEA2) on this subspace—reproducible, safe, hopeful.

Applications demonstrate Markham urban mastery (net-zero 2040 fusion co-gen scenarios) and scale to planetary Kardashev projection. Unity exceeds prior systems by enabling multi-objective trade-offs, gauge-guided confidence (sigma-mapped integrity), and hopeful convergence for civilization advancement.

Keywords: unity-sum regimes, Hilbert space truncation, bounded determinism, gauge-guided confidence, multi-objective evolution, planetary decision mastery

2. Mathematical Foundation

2.1 Hilbert Space Formalism
Let H be the infinite-dimensional separable Hilbert space of all conceivable regimes r_i (energy, mobility, economic, social, environmental, planetary, mythological...). Orthonormal basis {|r_i>}.

Decision state:
|psi> = sum_i w_i |r_i> ,  <psi|psi> = 1

Unity-sum invariant enforces exact normalization:
sum_i |w_i|^2 = 1

Inner product <r_i | r_j> = delta_ij (orthonormal); extended correlations via objective overlap.

Proof of Completeness (Hilbert Inheritance):
Line 1: H = l^2 complete under norm ||psi||_2 = sqrt(sum |w_i|^2) < infinity.
Line 2: Unity simplex S = {psi in H : sum |w_i| = 1, w_i >= 0}.
Line 3: Cauchy sequence {psi^n} in S converges in H to psi.
Line 4: Pointwise w_i^n -> w_i.
Line 5: sum |w_i^n| = 1 -> sum |w_i| = 1 (limit preserves).
Line 6: w_i >= 0 preserved.
Line 7: psi in S -> S closed -> complete.

Banach analog (l^1 norm ||psi||_1 = sum |w_i|): same argument.

2.2 Gauge Operator & Significance
Gauge G_r : r_i -> [0,1],
G_r_i = p * c * (1 - e)
p = provenance, c = completeness, e = normalized entropy.

Significance threshold sigma.

2.3 Truncation to Significance
Truncation projector T_sigma:
T_sigma |psi> = (sum_{G_r_i >= sigma} w_i |r_i>) / ||...||_2

Proof T_sigma Bounded Projection:

Line 2: ||T_sigma psi||_2 = 1 (renormalize).
Line 3: T_sigma^2 = T_sigma (idempotent).
Line 4: ||T_sigma|| <= 1 (contraction in significant subspace).
Line 5: Determinism: Fixed sigma + gauge -> unique truncated state.

2.4 Bounded Deterministic Evolution
