# Proof Burden and Misunderstanding Register

**Status:** Working conference-preparation register
**Purpose:** Identify what HUF must still prove, what outsiders are most likely to misunderstand, and how to answer in a way that reduces confusion without hiding uncertainty.
**Use:** Battle card, rewrite filter, presentation discipline, second-room reference.

---

## Why This Document Exists

HUF now makes enough noise that even a small claim can trigger a large response. That is not a reason to hide. It is a reason to answer carefully, completely, and for the benefit of the inquisitor. Unknown is a step away from known. The rule is simple:

**Keep the sentence only if it reduces misunderstanding more than it increases proof burden.**

This register exists to apply that rule in practice. It is not a confession of weakness. It is an honesty instrument.

---

## Operating Principles

1. **Hide nothing.** If a gap exists, name it.
2. **Separate result from interpretation.** A measured effect is stronger than a beautiful explanation.
3. **Treat misunderstanding as predictable.** If a sentence invites the wrong inference, rewrite the sentence.
4. **Treat proof burden as real cost.** Every stronger claim incurs a heavier duty to show the work.
5. **Answer for the benefit of the questioner.** Even when the answer is "not yet known," the answer should improve the questioner's map of the terrain.

## Register

### 1. MC-4 / "Composition as Primary Monitoring Observable"

**Current claim:** HUF proposes that compositional structure can be treated as a primary monitoring signal, not only as a data condition requiring appropriate CoDa treatment. The novelty is operational use, not new mathematics.

**Proof burden:** Show that this monitoring framing is not already present in existing CoDa practice under another name. This is the prior-art boundary. If prior art already frames composition as a primary operational monitoring category, MC-4 is not novel in the claimed sense.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "HUF claims new mathematics" or "HUF is trying to replace CoDa."

**Safe wording:** "The mathematics is not new; the monitoring application may be."

**Answer discipline:** Do not defend novelty as identity. Defend it as a narrow operational proposal and invite correction if the literature already covers it.

---

### 2. Three Diagnostics: TV, Aitchison, and Coherence Residual

**Current claim:** The conference framing is a three-diagnostic instrument. Diagnostic disagreement is information, not failure. S016 evidence suggests the three diagnostics occupy different temporal bands in the present sample.

**Proof burden:** Show that the diagnostics are not merely redundant rephrasings of the same signal. The current burden is not to prove permanent orthogonality, but to show non-redundancy in the present sample and explain what each diagnostic contributes.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "You are mixing incompatible metrics incoherently."

**Safe wording:** "The three diagnostics are used diagnostically, not fused into one truth. Agreement is informative; disagreement is also informative."

**Answer discipline:** Do not overclaim independence. Say "non-redundant in the present sample" or "spectrally separated in the present sample," not "proven orthogonal."

---

### 3. Coherence Residual (CR)

**Current claim:** CR is emerging as the third diagnostic and may be sensitive to coupling structure, especially under shock and settling dynamics. The S016 two-phase model partially addresses CR grounding.

**Proof burden:** Show that CR is not arbitrary, redundant, or post-hoc. Formalize what CR measures, under what assumptions, and in which regimes the interpretation is valid. Partial grounding is not full grounding.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "CR is magic," "CR is a custom statistic with no theory," or "CR proves coupling."

**Safe wording:** "CR currently supports a coupling interpretation in the present analyses; that interpretation is still being formalized."

**Answer discipline:** Present CR as a live measurement question, not as a settled theorem. Keep the two-phase model, but attach caveats immediately.

### 4. Null Model and Significance Language

**Current claim:** The packet reports a Germany EMBER result and explicitly warns reviewers to press on the null-model specification before accepting the p-value as a conventional significance result.

**Proof burden:** Specify and justify the null. Until the null is formalized, significance claims carry real proof burden.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "HUF is using p-values more strongly than the method supports."

**Safe wording:** "The reported p-value is provisional and should be read as part of an exploratory detection protocol until the null model is formalized."

**Answer discipline:** Say exactly how far the inference goes and no farther.

### 5. Zeros

**Current claim:** HUF treats zeros as events first and mathematical problems second.

**Proof burden:** Show a disciplined protocol for distinguishing ecological or structural zeros from missingness, censoring, reporting artifacts, and politically induced silence. Saying "zero is an event" is not yet enough.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "HUF has no real zero handling" or "HUF is trying to bypass standard CoDa zero problems with rhetoric."

**Safe wording:** "HUF treats zeros as events at the monitoring layer, but transform-layer handling still requires formal protocol."

**Answer discipline:** Never imply the event framing solves the full mathematical problem. It solves the order of operations, not the whole issue.

### 6. Loudspeaker Origin / Filter-Bank and Wave-Mechanics Bridge

**Current claim:** The engineering origin is no longer only biographical; S016 reframes it as methodological, with filter-bank, spectral, and impulse-response analyses contributing real findings.

**Proof burden:** If the loudspeaker-origin claims are presented as more than inspiration, they require formal mappings, not only compelling correspondences. The burden rises sharply when language shifts from "bridge" to "isomorphism."

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "The loudspeaker is doing metaphor work where proof should be," or "the speaker story is a narrative shortcut around CoDa."

**Safe wording:** "The loudspeaker is the source domain and engineering interpretation, not the primary mathematical proof."

**Answer discipline:** Use the loudspeaker as concept injector and coherence check. Keep it present, but at a distance. Let the instrument speak first.

### 7. Governance and Open-Loop Doctrine

**Current claim:** HUF insists on an open-loop posture: the instrument reads; the human decides. Governance protects statistics rather than replacing them.

**Proof burden:** Show that the governance layer actually constrains interpretation and action rather than merely describing good intentions.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "HUF is trying to smuggle governance or control doctrine into CoDa," or "governance is replacing statistical rigor."

**Safe wording:** "Governance protects the monitoring process; it does not replace geometry or inference."

**Answer discipline:** For Coimbra, keep governance in the second room unless directly asked. The first room is measurement.

### 8. Ramsar and Ecological Deployment

**Current claim:** Ramsar is treated as a proving ground and collaboration map, not as an already-earned deployment domain.

**Proof burden:** Validate on ecological data with real heterogeneity, dynamic zeros, reporting variation, and inconsistent methods.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "HUF is already ready for Ramsar," or "Coimbra is a recruitment drive for universal adoption."

**Safe wording:** "Ramsar is a future proving ground if the instrument survives mathematical scrutiny and ecological collaboration."

**Answer discipline:** Make Ramsar evident but quiet. Second room, not opening claim.

### 9. Documentation Volume / Meta-System Load

**Current claim:** The corpus has built extensive preparation, governance, error, and support documents. This is a strength if it helps calibration and a weakness if it becomes noise.

**Proof burden:** Show that the documentation supports the science rather than substituting for it.

**Most likely misunderstanding:** "This is over-engineered," "the documentation is compensating for lack of validation," or "the meta-system is larger than the result."

**Safe wording:** "The extra documents exist to expose failure modes and sharpen the scientific ask; they are not the ask itself."

**Answer discipline:** Lead with the cleanest mathematics, the working tool, and the real data. Everything else must justify its place.

## Red-Flag Phrases to Avoid

Avoid these unless formal proof is in hand:

- "This proves..."
- "This is isomorphic..."
- "This settles the debate..."
- "This shows universal validity..."
- "This is ready for institutional adoption..."
- "The loudspeaker independently derived CoDa..."

Prefer:

- "supports the interpretation that..."
- "is consistent with..."
- "is non-redundant in the present sample..."
- "remains exploratory..."
- "requires formalization..."
- "is a promising bridge, not yet a theorem..."

---

## Rewrite Filter

Before any sentence survives into the abstract, packet, talk, or demo, ask:

1. What proof burden does this sentence incur?
2. What misunderstanding is it most likely to trigger?
3. Does it reduce misunderstanding more than it increases proof burden?

If not, rewrite or remove it.

## One-Line Rule

**Tell the truth, hide nothing, and let every answer improve the questioner's map — even when the answer is not yet known.**
