## Statement

The project arc depends on preventing three contamination paths at once:

**Path 1 — Data contaminates theory:** Noisy, sparse, shuffled, or boundary-distorted data creates false theoretical conclusions. The cure: operating envelope, resolution classes, null-model testing.

**Path 2 — Theory contaminates method:** An attractive theory tempts premature corrections, forced unification, or overclaiming. The analogy is treated as proof. The cure: promotion ladder, HIVP regression chain, separation of science signal from process control.

**Path 3 — Method contaminates data:** Wrong averaging, bad zero handling, overaggressive correction injects artifacts into the readout. The cure: geometric mean (proven), multiplicative zero replacement (documented), F17 separation from core observable.

## The Integrity Rule

**Prefer a weaker but cleaner claim over a stronger contaminated one.**

This is the central discipline. It means:
- The Bell test stays at L0 (VERY HIGH contamination risk) despite being numerically striking
- The PLL correspondence stays at L0 (HIGH risk — analogy ahead of proof) despite being emotionally resonant
- The nuclear corridor stays at L0 (MEDIUM risk) despite being exciting
- The quantum isomorphisms stay at L0 (HIGH risk) despite being beautiful

Clean claims are promoted. Contaminated claims wait.

### Path 1 Formalisation — Data Contaminates Theory

The null-model test for EITT: generate random Dirichlet walks on S^D, compute delta_M. Expected result: delta_M >> 1% (measured: 6.65%). If a real dataset produces delta_M comparable to random walks, the EITT claim is falsified for that domain.

The shuffle test: permute the temporal ordering of a passing series. Expected result: delta_M >> original (temporal autocorrelation destroyed). If shuffling does not increase delta_M, the invariance is trivial (not dependent on temporal structure).
