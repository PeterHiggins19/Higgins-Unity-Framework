INTERPRETATION
══════════════

1. TWO NEW DOMAIN CONFIRMATIONS
   BackBlaze (hardware degradation, K=4): HOLDS at 0.0275% — first non-energy test
   Financial (120-stock price-level portfolio, K=9; NOT market-cap weighted): HOLDS at 0.0797% — second non-energy test
   Both show monotonic decimation ladders. Both have near-perfect zero-sum closure.

2. EITT FAILURE IS DIAGNOSTIC (Gold/Silver)
   The gold/silver ratio has 104% entropy range over 338 years — massive secular drift.
   At SHORT windows (2:1 = 0.12%, 4:1 = 0.44%) EITT holds.
   At LONG windows (50:1 = 6.7%) it fails.
   → EITT breakdown marks the scale at which stationarity breaks.
   → The failure is informative: it tells you WHERE the system's structural memory ends.
   Monotonicity only 56% over 50 years — the ratio swings between gold and silver
   dominance decade to decade. No temporal persistence → no EITT.

3. ENERGY MARGINAL — ACTIVE STRUCTURAL TRANSITION
   Global electricity generation is in the middle of the fastest energy transition
   in history. Solar: 0% → 7.1%, Wind: 0.2% → 8.4%, Coal: -6.6 pp, Nuclear: -9 pp.
   At 5:1 compression: 0.08% (holds). At 12:1: 1.04% (marginal).
   The secular trend overwhelms invariance at longer compression windows.
   Country-level tests (Ember per-country) may hold better since they capture
   individual trajectories without mixing fast-transitioning and slow countries.

4. WHAT PREDICTS EITT HOLDING
   Pattern across all 4 domains:
   - Low base entropy range → EITT holds (BackBlaze 1.0%, Financial 1.8%)
   - High base entropy range → EITT fails (Commodities 104%, Energy 21.7%)
   - High balance monotonicity → EITT holds (BackBlaze B2: 100%)
   - Low balance monotonicity → EITT fails (Commodities B1: 56%)

   Hypothesis: EITT requires temporal autocorrelation in the balance coordinates.
   When the system's structural trajectory is smooth and persistent, geometric-mean
   decimation preserves entropy. When the trajectory oscillates or undergoes
   secular drift, the geometric mean smears across structural regimes.
