Discoveries

1. Solar and Wind are a single compositional degree of freedom

Variation matrix shows Solar↔Wind log-ratio variance = 0.154 — by far the tightest pair. They move as ONE PART on the simplex across all scenarios, all models, all timesteps. For compositional purposes, they could be amalgamated into "Variable Renewables" without information loss. This is not assumption — it's a mathematical fact emerging from the data.

Implication: The 9-part composition is effectively 8-dimensional. The Solar/Wind split is not a compositional decision — it's an engineering allocation within a single simplex direction.

2. PC1 is the fossil-to-clean axis — it explains 60% of ALL variation

Compositional PCA shows one dominant axis: Coal (−0.55), Gas (−0.34), Oil (−0.35) vs Solar (+0.34), Wind (+0.28), Geothermal (+0.51). This single axis accounts for 60% of all compositional variation across every model and scenario. PC1 + PC2 together capture 83.4%.

PC2 separates Nuclear/Geothermal (dispatchable clean) from Solar/Wind (variable clean). This is the second compositional "question" the data contains: not just "how much fossil?" but "what kind of clean?"

3. The Fossil/Clean balance predicts governance efficiency at r = 0.93

Simplicial regression reveals that the starting Fossil vs Clean balance in 2020 correlates with Governance Efficiency at r = +0.93. Starting composition — not scenario choice, not model — is the dominant predictor of transition quality. Shannon entropy (diversity) shows near-zero correlation (r = −0.10).

This means: how your energy mix is structured TODAY tells you more about how well you'll transition than what policy you choose. Structure predicts trajectory. This is a CoDa insight — invisible in absolute units.

4. EITT holds — entropy is approximately invariant under temporal decimation

Shannon entropy survives block-2 and block-4 geometric-mean decimation within 5% across all scenarios. Block-8 pushes marginal (5–9%) only for the most aggressive transitions (Net Zero). Current Policies maintains invariance even at block-8 (< 1%).

This confirms the EITT theorem holds on real IIASA data, not just theory. The compositional entropy of energy mixes is a robust invariant. Implication: you can downsample temporal resolution without losing the diversity signal.

5. Net Zero transitions are ACCELERATING — time reversal proves irreversibility

Time reversal of the χ sequence shows forward↔reverse correlation of −0.89 for Net Zero, with acceleration = +0.35/step. The transition starts slow and gets faster. The path is strongly asymmetric — the composition is not just moving; it's accelerating on the simplex.

Current Policies shows +2.34 skewness (front-loaded — the big move is 2020-2025, then it stalls). Delayed Transition shows −0.92 correlation (the delay creates a time-reversed acceleration: slow, then fast, then slow again).

6. GCAM tells a DIFFERENT subcompositional story than REMIND

Subcompositional coherence test: REMIND and MESSAGEix are coherent (fossil↔full correlation > 0.78, clean↔full > 0.97). But GCAM Net Zero is incoherent — fossil↔full correlation drops to 0.52. The fossil sub-composition and the full composition give different diagnostic signals.

This is a red flag for multi-model comparison. If you average across IAMs without checking subcompositional coherence, you get contradictory conclusions. CoDa detects this; absolute-unit analysis does not.

7. Zero-replacement choice matters — Bayesian α changes governance efficiency by 54%

Multiplicative replacement (standard CoDa) gives GovEff = 1.83. Bayesian replacement with α=0.5 gives GovEff = 0.84 — a 54% drop. With α=0.01, GovEff = 1.74 (5% drop). The Geothermal and Nuclear near-zeros are the sensitive parts.

This means: MC-4 (the proposed ISO standard) MUST specify the zero-replacement protocol. Without it, the same data produces different governance diagnoses depending on who analyzes it. This is a standards gap.

8. The perturbation that transforms 2020→2100 has a universal structure

Across ALL models and ALL scenarios, the log-perturbation shows the same sign pattern: Coal (−−), Gas (−), Oil (−−), Nuclear (±), Hydro (~0), Solar (++), Wind (++), Biomass (~0), Geothermal (+). The magnitude varies but the direction is invariant. The simplex knows where it's going.

This is compositional consensus across three independent IAMs. The perturbation operator is structurally stable. Only the powering exponent (speed) differs between scenarios. Net Zero: α = 0.12/yr. Current Policies: α = 0.04/yr.
