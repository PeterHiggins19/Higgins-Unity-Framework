Methodology & Data Notes

Data source: Backblaze Drive Stats Q1 2025, daily snapshot 2025-01-01 (trimmed export, 104,749 drives).
Analysis method: Systematic sampling at 20+ offset positions across the sorted CSV to derive model distribution, SMART health profiles, and datacenter allocation. SMART attribute column positions mapped from Drive_Stats_Schema_Current.csv (198 columns, Q2 2024 schema).
Models identified: 12+ unique models including SSDs (Crucial CT250MX500, Seagate BarraCuda SSD), laptop HDDs (ST500LM012), and enterprise HDDs (Seagate Exos, HGST Ultrastar, Toshiba MG07).
Key finding: Zero failures on this snapshot date. Fleet age is the primary risk vector — Seagate ST8000DM002 drives average 74,000+ power-on hours (8.5 years). HGST 12TB drives are the newest cohort (~5,000–50,000 POH).
Limitations: Single-day snapshot; failure rates require multi-day analysis. Model counts are estimates from stratified sampling, not exact counts.
