## 10 HARDEST QUESTIONS — HONEST ANSWERS

### 1. "What is actually new here?"

The monitoring architecture. The simplex is yours. Nobody has built a continuous monitoring protocol on it with perturbation-based drift detection. If someone has, show me — that defeats my claim and I'll accept it. I brought four defeat paths for exactly that purpose.

### 2. "Why no log-ratio transforms?"

Because I haven't learned to implement them properly yet. The framework operates on raw proportions. Expressing the same trajectories in ILR coordinates and comparing drift detection sensitivity is the first thing I want to do with your help.

### 3. "How do you handle zeros?"

Structural zeros are flagged as genuine absence. For everything else, I need guidance from this community. That's one of my six open gaps.

### 4. "Where's the null model?"

It isn't specified. The p-value comes from a Pettitt test without a formal compositional null. Specifying a principled null model for compositional time series is one of the main reasons I'm standing in this room.

### 5. "Is this subcompositionally coherent?"

I haven't tested it. I can test it empirically with the data I have. I'd appreciate hearing what the expected behaviour should be so I know what to look for.

### 6. "Isn't this just CoDa applied to energy data?"

If it is, that defeats my claim. Show me the existing compositional monitoring protocol with carrier-level drift detection and I'll withdraw. What I believe is new is the monitoring architecture — the distinction between observation and governance, and cross-domain portability.

### 7. "Why both TV and Aitchison distance?"

I started with TV from information theory. Aitchison distance is the simplex-native metric. They agree on regime boundaries and differ on fine structure. I'm keeping both until someone helps me understand why they diverge.

### 8. "How is this different from Dirichlet monitoring?"

Dirichlet is parametric. This is model-free. Which detects better is an empirical question. My data is public — I'd be glad to see the comparison.

### 9. "Why should we take an independent researcher seriously?"

Judge the work. The data is public, the code is public, the claim has four defeat paths, and I'm here because Professor Egozcue invited me after reviewing the packet.

### 10. "Did AI help with this?"

Yes. Six AI systems as research tools. The physical insight and 40 years of operational experience are mine. The AIs helped me find your vocabulary, check my mathematics, and prepare for this conversation. I disclosed it because honest science requires it.
