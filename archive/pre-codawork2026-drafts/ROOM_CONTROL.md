# CoDaWork 2026 — Room-Control Strategy

**Source:** Copilot collective review, April 4, 2026.
**Classification:** Rough diamond — conference preparation material.

---

## Controlling Mathematicians

Mathematicians test you on definitions, coherence, invariants, edge cases, logical consistency. They don't care about the story — they care about whether the story commutes.

**Your posture:** Give them invariants, not enthusiasm.

**Move M1 — Pre-empt the coherence test**
Say early: "Everything I show today respects subcompositional coherence. Where CoDa and HUF disagree, we treat that disagreement as a diagnostic signal."

**Move M2 — Use their language**
Terms to use: "invariant under closure", "isometry in Aitchison geometry", "balance coordinates", "perturbation vs powering". This tells them you're not a tourist.

**Move M3 — When challenged, narrow the scope**
If they push on something exotic (signed CoDa, complex CoDa, SBP design), say: "That's outside the scope of the calibration study. We're presenting the union in its narrowest, most defensible form." Mathematicians respect scope discipline more than breadth.

## Controlling Engineers

Engineers test you on architecture, failure modes, stored energy, signal flow, what breaks under load. They don't care about elegance — they care about whether it works.

**Your posture:** Give them the block diagram and the failure modes.

**Move E1 — Lead with the GOV/CLS separation**
Say: "HUF-GOV is a phase discriminator — stateless, open loop. HUF-CLS is the PLL — closed loop, stored energy. Only GOV belongs in scientific monitoring." This resolves 80% of engineering objections before they form.

**Move E2 — Show the error catalogue**
Engineers love failure modes. Say: "We mapped 25 error sources. Each has a detection test and a governance action."

**Move E3 — Use their metaphors**
Terms to use: "stored energy", "loop stability", "aliasing", "dimensionality change as a topology shift".

---

## Controlling Skeptics

Skeptics test you on overreach, ambition, novelty claims, whether you're selling something. They don't care about the instrument — they care about whether you're grounded.

**Your posture:** Give them humility and calibration.

**Move S1 — Say the line that disarms the room**
"This is not a new framework. This is a calibration study." This is the single most powerful sentence in the entire presentation.

**Move S2 — Admit failure conditions**
Say: "If the union fails, it fails. We're here to test the joint, not defend it." Skeptics relax when you show you're not selling.

**Move S3 — Anchor everything in CoDa**
Say: "CoDa perfected the geometry. HUF just watches it move." This frames HUF as a complement, not a competitor.

---

## Universal Tactics

**Tactic U1 — Ask them questions**
People don't attack what they helped build.

**Tactic U2 — Use the audit trail as the anchor**
Every time the room drifts, bring it back to: drift, disagreement, shocks, zeros, dimensionality. These are universal concepts.

**Tactic U3 — Never defend the instrument**
Always defend the calibration study. It's unassailable.

**Tactic U4 — Silence is your ally**
After a strong point, pause. Let them fill the silence with agreement.

## Three Hardest Scenarios

**Scenario A — A mathematician challenges the three-diagnostic protocol**
You say: "Disagreement is a diagnostic signal. We don't fuse the metrics — we compare them."

**Scenario B — An engineer insists HUF is a PLL**
You say: "HUF-GOV has no stored energy. A PLL cannot exist without stored energy. Therefore HUF-GOV is not a PLL."

**Scenario C — A skeptic says the union is too ambitious**
You say: "We agree. That's why we're presenting the narrowest possible version — the calibration study."

## The One Line That Controls the Entire Room

Say this once, slowly:

> "CoDa perfected the geometry of compositions at rest; HUF discovered what happens when compositions move."

This line is the thesis. It is the union. It is the room-control anchor.

**Emphasize:** error catalogue, diagnostic protocol, dimensionality-aware time series, open-loop doctrine, audit trail of events.

**Avoid:** "new framework", "replacement for CoDa", "unified theory".

**Core framing:** "This is a monitoring instrument built on CoDa geometry, tested with HUF doctrine, and presented as a calibration study."
