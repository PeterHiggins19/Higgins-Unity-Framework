## Devil's-Advocate Questions (DA-01 through DA-08)

### DA-01 — "Isn't HUF just MEWMA with different branding?"

**Why they ask:** CRPT-007 explicitly states that MEWMA-CoDa and HUF are converging on the same instrument. They will test whether you understand the distinction.

**Your response:** "No — MEWMA is a control chart with stored energy. HUF-GOV is a stateless phase discriminator. The union works because each solves the other's blind spot. MEWMA has statistical optimization; HUF has governance."

---

### DA-02 — "Your instrument breaks when a carrier goes to zero. Isn't that a fatal flaw?"

**Why they ask:** Zero replacement is a core CoDa issue. E-03 in the entanglement catalogue.

**Your response:** "In HUF, a zero is not a mathematical inconvenience — it is an event. We detect the event before applying any CoDa transform. The union governs the tension: treat zeros as events first, mathematical problems second."

---

### DA-03 — "Why should CoDa adopt governance concepts? We already have statistical discipline."

**Why they ask:** CoDa is mathematically mature and may resist governance framing.

**Your response:** "Governance is not replacing statistics — it is protecting them. CoDa handles geometry at rest; HUF handles motion and monitoring. The union is about monitoring, not replacing CoDa math."

---

### DA-04 — "Isn't HUF just a PLL in disguise?"

**Why they ask:** Jack's objection is the canonical version of this.

**Your response:** "No. HUF-GOV is explicitly not a PLL. It is a phase discriminator — stateless, open loop. Only HUF-CLS is a PLL, and CLS is not part of the scientific instrument."

---

### DA-05 — "Your analyzer uses TV and Aitchison distance. Isn't that mixing incompatible metrics?"

**Why they ask:** Dual-metric monitoring is new and will be challenged.

**Your response:** "The dual metric is a diagnostic, not a fusion. When the two disagree, that's information — not an error."

---

### DA-06 — "Why should we trust your 17-error catalogue? Where is the validation?"

**Why they ask:** CoDa researchers value published literature and peer review.

**Your response:** "Because it is the first calibration study of its kind. No existing CoDa tool has a published failure-mode catalogue. The catalogue is not a claim of correctness — it is a map of what breaks."

---

### DA-07 — "Isn't this too ambitious? Why not stay within CoDa?"

**Why they ask:** They want to see if you understand scope discipline.

**Your response:** "We agree. CONJ-001 states: the winning horse is the basic union, not the exotic extensions. We are presenting a calibration study, not a revolution."

---

### DA-08 — "What if the union fails? What if the metrics disagree too often?"

**Why they ask:** They want to test your humility and scientific discipline.

**Your response:** "Then the union fails. The responsible way to present a combined instrument is not to celebrate its capabilities but to calibrate its failure modes. We are here to test the claim, not to defend it."
