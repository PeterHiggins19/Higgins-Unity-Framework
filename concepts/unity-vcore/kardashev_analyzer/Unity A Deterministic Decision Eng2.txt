Abstract
Complex decision systems—from municipal net-zero pathways to planetary resource allocation—require frameworks balancing infinite possibilities with practical, traceable outcomes. Traditional optimization suffers stochastic noise or unbounded drift; performance hierarchies (e.g., Bhutan GPMS) excel alignment but lack dynamic trade-offs.

We introduce Unity, a novel primitive operating as a deterministic projector in infinite-dimensional Hilbert space. The full possibility space comprises all conceivable regimes (energy, mobility, economic, social, environmental, planetary) as basis vectors. Unity enforces exact normalization (unity-sum invariant) and determinism via forced truncation to significance: Low-gauge (uncertain) dimensions discarded, projecting to finite significant subspace. Evolution is bounded hill-climb or Pareto search (NSGA-II/SPEA2) on this subspace—reproducible, safe, hopeful.

Inspired by surface minimization in physical networks [1], Unity extends to decision manifolds—reciprocity enables any-to-any navigation. Applications demonstrate Markham urban mastery (net-zero 2040 fusion co-gen scenarios) and scale to planetary Kardashev projection. Unity exceeds prior systems by enabling multi-objective trade-offs, gauge-guided confidence (sigma-mapped integrity), and hopeful convergence for civilization advancement.

The escalation of global challenges—climate resilience, resource allocation, urban growth—demands decision engines capable of navigating infinite-dimensional possibility spaces while delivering bounded, reproducible outcomes. Stochastic methods introduce noise; rigid hierarchies (e.g., performance agreements) lack adaptability.

Inspired by Meng et al. [1] demonstrating surface minimization governs physical network branching (trifurcations/orthogonal sprouts via high-dim Feynman diagrams), we generalize to decision space: Infinite regimes → manifold surface → truncation to significant → bounded evolution.

Unity addresses this as a Hilbert space engine: All regimes form an infinite basis {|r_i>}. Decision states |psi> = sum w_i |r_i>, with unity-sum constraint sum |w_i|^2 = 1. Determinism emerges from forced truncation to significance: A gauge operator G_r quantifies regime confidence (provenance + completeness + inverse entropy). Dimensions with G_r < sigma (threshold, e.g., 0.9) are truncated—projecting to finite significant subspace.

Evolution on this subspace is bounded (capped cycles, small mutations) and multi-objective (Pareto fronts via NSGA-II [2]/SPEA2 [3]). Reciprocity enables any start to any goal (forward planning/reverse diagnosis).
